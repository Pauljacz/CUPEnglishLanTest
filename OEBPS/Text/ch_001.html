Comment on purpose and effect
A response is always better if it does more than quote a fact or detail. A good response comments on the purpose or effect ofthe detail, and then adds a comment on what the reader thinks or feels about it.

Getting sorted:

These questions get you to move from understanding to interpretation. This means using what you’ve learned from the text to develop some ideas of your own about them. It’s about showing that you can take the content of the text – facts, ideas, feelings and relate them to your own life or to life at large. Obviously, your interpretation will be based on your own ideas, values and attitudes.
